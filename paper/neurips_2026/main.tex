\begin{abstract}
Industrial computer-aided design (CAD) demands the fusion of complex spatial understanding, parametric computation, and program synthesis: producing executable code that satisfies industry tolerances, feature detail, and the unwritten engineering conventions that distinguish a manufacturable part from primitive geometry. Frontier multimodal language models show nascent capability along each of these axes individually --- 3D and spatial reasoning, multi-step reasoning, and code generation --- yet their joint alignment for industrial CAD remains under-evaluated. We present \textbf{BenchCAD}, a unified, capability-decomposed evaluation framework whose tasks correspond to stages of practising CAD-engineering work (read a drawing, interrogate a parametric program, modify it under instruction). The dataset contains 17{,}900 execution-verified CadQuery parts across 106 industrial part families anchored to 47 ISO/DIN/EN/ASME/IEC specification codes (twist drills, bevel gears, compression springs, roller chains, threaded fittings), exercising 45+ distinct CadQuery operations including helical sweeps, lofts, twist-extrudes, and parametric involute-gear construction. Four task categories --- image-to-code, image- and code-conditioned numeric QA, and instruction-guided editing with verified before/after pairs and a non-target preservation metric --- decompose the image$\to$code pipeline so that score differences between paired tasks isolate which sub-capability (visual perception, geometric-to-parametric abstraction, or code synthesis) drives a model's remaining gap. Across 10+ frontier models we identify three diagnostic phenomena: a 15--20 point \emph{Vision Bottleneck} between code- and image-conditioned QA that persists under chain-of-thought, parameter scaling from 1B to 78B, and view-count augmentation; a 64\% \emph{Edit Leakage} rate on nominally successful edits where unrelated parametric features are silently corrupted; and an \emph{Op Vocabulary Gap} where matched-compute SFT on sketch+extrude data alone yields zero recall on essential operations until BenchCAD-Synth enters the training mix. A consistent dichotomy emerges: today's frontier models recover the outer envelope of an industrial part but not the parametric structure underlying it. BenchCAD complements existing CAD-LM training corpora by providing the evaluation-side properties they do not --- industrial-family taxonomy, standard-table anchoring, verified edit pairs, paired-task capability decomposition, and rotation- and scale-invariant scoring. Released under CC-BY-4.0 at \url{huggingface.co/BenchCAD}.
\end{abstract}
