\begin{abstract}
Computer-aided design (CAD) demands a difficult fusion of complex spatial understanding, parametric computation, and program synthesis: producing executable CAD code that meets industry tolerances and feature detail requires a model to read 3D geometry, abstract it into parametric structure, and emit syntactically valid programs. Frontier multimodal language models show nascent capability along each of these axes individually --- 3D and spatial reasoning, multi-step reasoning, and code generation --- yet their joint alignment for industrial CAD remains under-evaluated, and no public benchmark currently measures these capabilities in a systematic, capability-decomposed way that reflects how industry actually uses CAD: most components are variants of a small set of standard part families, and most engineering work is editing an existing parametric program rather than authoring a greenfield one. We present \textbf{BenchCAD}, a unified, capability-decomposed evaluation framework for large-model CAD reasoning, designed to evaluate and help strengthen these emerging capabilities toward industry-grade work. The dataset contains 17{,}900 execution-verified CadQuery parts across 106 industrial part families (49\% anchored to 47 ISO/DIN/EN/ASME/IEC specification codes covering twist drills, bevel gears, compression springs, roller chains, and threaded fittings), exercising 45+ distinct CadQuery operations including helical sweeps, lofts, twist-extrudes, and parametric involute-gear construction. Atop this dataset we define four evaluation task categories --- image-to-code generation, image- and code-conditioned numeric QA, and instruction-guided editing with verified before/after pairs and a non-target preservation metric --- where score differences across paired image/code tasks isolate which sub-capability (visual perception, geometric-to-parametric abstraction, or code synthesis) contributes most to a model's remaining gap. Across 10+ frontier models we identify three diagnostic phenomena that highlight concrete capability dimensions for further research and training innovation: a 15--20 point \emph{Vision Bottleneck} between code- and image-conditioned numeric QA that persists under chain-of-thought, parameter scaling from 1B to 78B, and view-count augmentation; a 64\% \emph{Edit Leakage} rate on nominally successful edits where unrelated parametric features are silently corrupted; and an \emph{Op Vocabulary Gap} where matched-compute SFT on sketch+extrude data alone yields zero recall on essential operations until BenchCAD-Synth enters the training mix. These findings map both the current state of frontier capability and the focused improvements needed to reach industry-grade CAD generation. Released under CC-BY-4.0 at \url{huggingface.co/BenchCAD}.
\end{abstract}
