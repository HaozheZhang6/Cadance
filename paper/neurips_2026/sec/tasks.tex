\section{Benchmark Tasks and Capability Decomposition}
\label{sec:tasks}

\subsection{The Image$\to$Code Capability Chain}
\label{sec:capability}

BenchCAD's four task categories correspond to stages of practising CAD-engineering work: an engineer reads an existing drawing or part (\texttt{img2cq} reconstruction), interrogates dimensions and operations on either the rendered geometry or the source program (\texttt{qa\_img}/\texttt{qa\_code}), and modifies an existing parametric program in response to a request (\texttt{edit\_img}/\texttt{edit\_code}).
A single end-to-end metric on an image-to-code task reports \emph{whether} a model succeeded but not \emph{which capability} failed.
We decompose the image$\to$code task into a four-level capability hierarchy (Table~\ref{tab:capability}; visualised in Figure~\ref{fig:capability_hierarchy}):
\emph{$L_1$ Holistic Visual Recognition} --- recognise the part family from images and integrate multi-view input into a coherent volumetric understanding;
\emph{$L_2$ CAD Operations Understanding} --- read existing CadQuery and identify which operation creates which feature, in the right execution order;
\emph{$L_3$ Industrial Parametric Abstraction} --- abstract observed geometry into the parametric structure underlying it, including the engineering conventions and standard-table relations a manufacturable part must respect;
and \emph{$L_4$ Spatial/Code Reasoning} --- compose the lower levels into syntactically valid, executable parametric code.
BenchCAD's tasks exercise known subsets of $\{L_1, L_2, L_3, L_4\}$, so score \emph{differences} across paired tasks isolate the bottleneck level.
The central contrast is the gap between \texttt{qa\_img} (which exercises $L_1$+$L_3$) and \texttt{qa\_code} (which exercises $L_2$+$L_3$) on matched prompts: this gap measures the cost of replacing direct code access with visual recognition --- isolating $L_1$ failure --- and is named the \textbf{Holistic Spatial and Detailing Deficit} (\S\ref{sec:exp_capability}).
Two further contrasts apply: \texttt{qa\_code} low absolute $\Rightarrow$ $L_2$ bottleneck (\textbf{CAD Operational Blindspot}); \texttt{edit\_*} non-target-preservation failures $\Rightarrow$ $L_3$ bottleneck (\textbf{Industrial Common Sense Gap}); \texttt{img2cq} and \texttt{edit\_*} compose all four levels into the $L_4$ synthesis output.

\subsection{Task Definitions and Scoring}

\paragraph{Five tasks, four categories.} \texttt{img2cq} (image-to-code): four canonical orthographic views $\to$ CadQuery code; scored by $\mathrm{Pass}_{\mathrm{IoU}\geq 0.5}$ under the rotation-invariant cube symmetry group, with mean Chamfer distance, invalidity ratio, and Feature-F1 as auxiliary metrics.
\texttt{qa\_img}/\texttt{qa\_code} (visual / code-conditioned numeric QA): a 2{,}400-question per-family bank asking only ratios and integer counts (never absolute mm) for scale invariance, scored by relative error $\leq 5\%$ for ratios and exact match for integers; the paired design (each question evaluated under both modalities) directly isolates $L_1$ visual recognition from $L_2$ code/op understanding.
\texttt{edit\_img}/\texttt{edit\_code} (image-conditioned / text-only editing): a 748-pair instruction-guided edit set spanning dimensional/additive/subtractive/multi-step categories, scored as Pass@1 = $\mathbb{1}[\mathrm{IoU}(\text{edited},\text{target})\geq 0.99 \wedge \mathrm{Preserve}(\text{edited},\text{orig})\geq 0.99]$ requiring both target success and non-target preservation; we additionally report the disaggregated Industrial Common Sense Gap rate (\S\ref{sec:exp_edit}).
The headline aggregate is the macro-mean across the five tasks.
Question bank construction, edit-pair protocol, rotation-invariant IoU, and a scale-invariance ablation appear in Appendices~\ref{app:qbank}, \ref{app:edit_protocol}, \ref{app:iou}, and \ref{app:ablation} respectively.
